Zell am See, the lake at the foot of the Alps

Drinking-water clarity, 4 km long, ringed by snow caps in early June. Apartment is in town — drop bags, walk to the Strandbad in five minutes, swim before lunch.

Friday tips

  • Lake boat cruise ≈ €12 — 1 hour, sunset.
  • 13 km loop on foot if the legs aren't tired yet.
  • HOFER on Brucker Bundesstraße for picnic supplies.

How to get there

ÖBB direct trains Vienna Hbf → Zell am See, ~5 h, €30 one way with Sparschiene. Apartment is 8 min walk from the station.

Sigmund-Thun-Klamm — Kaprun

Wooden walkways bolted into limestone cliffs above a roaring meltwater torrent. 320 m long, depths to 30 m. One easy hour. The racers' legs stay fresh.

Saturday morning

  • Bus 660 from Postplatz to Kaprun ≈ €5.
  • Entry ≈ €5, open from 08:30.
  • Continue uphill to the Kapruner reservoirs if time allows.

Where

South end of Kaprun village, signposted from the parking lot. 15 min from the bus stop.

See Saturday plan
KlammKaprun, Salzburg

Kitzsteinhorn — 3,029 m

Cable car from Kaprun to "Top of Salzburg". 360° Eastern Alps panorama, Schmiedinger glacier under your feet. Eat the packed lunch on the terrace.

Money tip

  • Return ticket ≈ €40. Worth it on a clear day, skip if cloudy.
  • Budget alt: free walk to the Wasserfallboden reservoir.
  • Wind layer + sunglasses non-negotiable.

Logistics

Cable car valley station is in Kaprun, 8 km from Zell am See. Bus 660 runs every 30 min. Last lift down: 16:30.

Fuscher Törl — 2,428 m

The race finish. 27.3 km, 1,672 m of climbing, max 12 %. Fan squad drives the Hochalpenstraße at dawn and meets the racers with hot coffee and a mountain picnic.

Race day notes

  • Toll: €46.50 / car ÷ 5 people = €9.30 each.
  • Start 07:00 in Bruck, ETA at the top ≈ 08:30–09:30.
  • 5–10 °C at the finish, even in June. Layers + rain jacket.

Beyond the finish

Continue south on the Hochalpenstraße to Kaiser-Franz-Josefs-Höhe (2,369 m). Pasterze glacier viewpoint, marmots, short walks.

A weekend in the Alps

Look closer to
the mountain.

Born in 1971, the Glocknerkönig is Austria's oldest open hillclimb — pros and amateurs share the same start line in Bruck at 07:00.

This is the 29th edition. The road itself is older still: opened in 1935, it carved the first paved route across the central Alps and stays closed every winter.

Hochalpenstraße

36 hairpins from Bruck to Heiligenblut. The road climbs through five vegetation zones and tops out near a 3,000-year-old glacier.

Tarmac since 1935, alpine pass since the Bronze Age. Open May to October only.
